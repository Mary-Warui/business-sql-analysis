# Business Intelligence Analysis Using SQL

## 📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes business license data to uncover patterns in business distribution, industry concentration, and market opportunities across different TownCentres. The analysis provides data-driven insights to support strategic decisions such as business expansion, location selection, and market entry.

### 1. What are the most common business categories?
- Identified dominant industries within the region 
- Analyzed overall market composition 

### 2. Which TownCentres have the highest business concentration?
- Determined key commercial hubs 
- Compared business density across regions 

### 3. How are businesses distributed between Commercial and Home-Based operations?

Key Insights 
### Industry Landscape (Top Business Categories)

<img width="859" height="641" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7570a6b7-135f-4733-9f62-8f0235677e47" />

The business landscape is strongly driven by skilled trades, particularly general and electrical contracting, indicating sustained demand in construction and infrastructure-related services.

Service-based industries such as driving schools, janitorial services, and immigration consulting are highly prevalent, reflecting consistent demand for essential and recurring services.

The presence of logistics and industrial sectors—including trucking, wholesale, and manufacturing—suggests a well-developed supply chain and commercial infrastructure.

Food services, particularly restaurants without alcohol licensing, show moderate representation, indicating a stable but regulated hospitality sector.

Professional services such as medical practitioners and immigration consultants highlight population-driven demand and suggest a diverse and growing community.

### Business Distribution by TownCentre
  <img width="989" height="642" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a0e3c8bb-e695-41cd-8acb-b1fd249859c3" />
  
Newton emerges as the primary business hub, with more than twice the number of businesses compared to South Surrey, indicating a highly concentrated and competitive commercial environment.

South Surrey represents a strong secondary market with significant business activity, suggesting continued economic growth.

Cloverdale, Guildford, and Whalley form a middle tier of business activity, offering a balance between opportunity and competition.

City Centre and Fleetwood show relatively lower business densities, which may present potential opportunities for new business entry due to reduced competition.

### Commercial vs Home-Based Business Structure


<img width="945" height="663" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e88fc4f6-73e1-4ea3-b731-d783d6e44bfc" />

distribution of business types varies significantly across TownCentres, revealing distinct economic structures.

City Centre has the highest proportion of commercial businesses (57.73%), indicating strong formal economic activity and infrastructure.

Newton, South Surrey, and Guildford exhibit a balanced mix of commercial and home-based businesses, suggesting mature and diversified local economies.

Fleetwood and Whalley are heavily dominated by home-based businesses, with Fleetwood showing the highest proportion (75.39%). This indicates a more residential-driven economy with a strong presence of small-scale and independent businesses.

Cloverdale shows a moderate lean toward home-based businesses, reflecting a transitional economic structure between residential and commercial activity.
